Section 01
The 18-Step End-to-End Workflow
Every step from the moment a sensor fires to the moment that knowledge is permanently wired into the system for every future technician.
Phase A — Detection & Visualization
Steps 1–4 · NextXR Digital Twin
01
Sensor

Sensor Alert — Temperature Spike

Digital Twin T+0 sec

IoT sensor reading indicates temperature spike: 85°C vs. normal 65°C on CNC Machine #47. Alert triggered, Digital Twin notified via IoT Gateway.

Output: Alert triggered · Twin notified
Agent: IoT Gateway
02
Ingest

Alert Ingestion & Classification

Digital Twin T+1 sec

Alert Router receives alert, identifies equipment (CNC Machine #47), classifies fault type and severity. Equipment status updated in the Twin.

Output: Equipment: FAULTY · Severity: HIGH
Agent: Alert Router Agent
03
3D View

Digital Twin Visualization

Digital Twin T+2–3 sec

Twin engine loads 3D model, displays equipment state in real-time, highlights the problem area in red. SOC Dashboard receives interactive visualization.

Output: Interactive 3D visualization · Problem area highlighted
Agent: Technician via SOC Dashboard
04
Scenario

Scenario Analysis & Generation

Digital Twin AUTOMIND T+3–5 sec

Scenario Engine queries knowledge base based on alert context. AUTOMIND orchestrates the diagnosis agent chain. Builds decision tree for repair with multi-step branches.

Output: Multi-step scenario · Decision branches ready
Agent: Scenario Generation Agent · Powered by AUTOMIND
Phase B — Training & Dispatch
Steps 5–9 · GoalCert Simulation Engine
05
Video

Training Video Generation (AI)

GoalCert T+5–12 sec

AI agent pulls from data lake, uses generative AI to synthesize a 5-minute training video specific to the CNC sensor replacement procedure.

Output: MP4 video · Voiceover · Step-by-step graphics
Agent: AI Content Generation Agent
06
LMS

Training Delivery to LMS

GoalCert T+12–15 sec

Training video and scenario steps are loaded into the LMS as a Just-In-Time Training course, ready for technician access.

Output: Course ID: JITC-CNC-SENSOR-REPLACE · Status: READY
Agent: LMS Content Agent
07
Notify

Technician Notification

GoalCert T+15 sec

Push notification sent to the nearest available technician with the course link, priority level, and equipment location via the Notification Engine.

Output: In-app notification · Course invitation sent
Agent: Dispatch/Notification Agent
08
Train

Technician Training & Skilling

GoalCert T+15–20 min

Technician completes the 5-minute JIT training on their mobile device. Assessment verified, competency confirmed before dispatch.

Output: Training: COMPLETE · Competency: VERIFIED
Agent: Technician (Field)
09
Dispatch

Technician Dispatched

GoalCert T+20–30 min

After training completion confirmed, technician dispatched to shop floor. Field navigation provided with exact equipment location.

Output: Dispatch confirmed · Technician en-route
Agent: Field Dispatch Agent
Phase C — AR-Guided Repair & Completion
Steps 10–13 · GoalCert AR + Digital Twin
10
AR Start

AR Remote Assistance Initiated

AR Session T+30 sec on-site

Technician arrives, opens AR app, streams live camera feed to SOC expert. AR session established with sub-100ms latency.

Output: AR session active · Video feed established · Latency <100ms
Agent: Technician + SOC Expert
11
AR Guide

Expert Guidance: Step-by-Step AR Overlay

AR Session T+0–40 min on-site

SOC Expert views live feed, annotates AR overlay with arrows and highlights for each repair step. Each step confirmed with visual proof captured.

Output: Step completion confirmed · Visual proof captured
Agent: Expert + Technician
12
Monitor

Interaction Monitoring & Reporting

AUTOMIND Continuous

AUTOMIND's analytics pipeline monitors entire interaction in parallel, captures video, logs step times, and tracks safety compliance throughout.

Output: Completion % · Time efficiency · Safety compliance
Agent: Monitoring & Analytics Agent · Powered by AUTOMIND
13
Complete

Maintenance Completion

Digital Twin T+35–40 min

Technician completes final repair step. Digital Twin receives sensor confirmation — temperature normalised. Equipment status updated to OPERATIONAL.

Output: Maintenance: COMPLETE · Equipment: OPERATIONAL
Agent: Technician + Scenario Agent
Phase D — Knowledge Capture & Future Reuse
Steps 14–18 · GoalCert Simulation Engine
14
Archive

AR Session Close & Archive

GoalCert T+40–41 min

AR session ended, session video archived to the data lake, indexed with equipment metadata for future retrieval.

Output: Video file stored · Indexed for future retrieval
Agent: AR Archive Agent
15
Report

Automated Report Generation

GoalCert T+41–50 min

AI synthesises a complete maintenance report from monitoring logs, session video, and compliance data. Includes executive summary and detailed step log.

Output: PDF Report · Executive summary · Safety verification
Agent: Report Generation Agent
16
Convert

Knowledge Conversion

GoalCert T+50–60 min

AI transforms the session into reusable knowledge: trimmed video clips, markdown procedure document, tagged for equipment type and fault signature.

Output: Knowledge Article · Video clips · Tagged for discovery
Agent: Content Conversion Agent
17
Index

Knowledge Base Indexing

GoalCert T+60–65 min

New knowledge article indexed in LMS search engine, added to equipment knowledge graph so it surfaces automatically for future relevant faults.

Output: Article discoverable · Added to knowledge graph
Agent: Knowledge Management Agent
18
Reuse

Future Reuse

GoalCert Future

Next technician encountering a similar fault gets recommended training surfaced automatically — 3 to 5 minutes instead of the original 20. Training time reduced 80%.

Output: Recommended training shown · 80% time reduction
Agent: Recommendation Agent

Every agent in the workflow — with their trigger, output, product ownership, and what happens if they fail.

IoT Alert Router

NextXR Digital Twin
Trigger Sensor reading exceeds threshold (85°C vs 65°C)
Output Equipment ID, fault classification, severity tag, Twin notification
Steps 1 → 2
Fallback Retry with exponential backoff (3x); escalate to manual SOC alert if still failing after 30 sec

Scenario Generation Agent

NextXR Digital Twin · Orchestrated by AUTOMIND
Trigger Alert classification complete, equipment context available
Output Multi-step scenario with decision branches, repair procedure tree
Steps 4
Fallback Load cached scenario template for equipment type; flag for human review

AI Video Generation Agent

GoalCert Simulation Engine
Trigger Scenario steps confirmed, data lake query complete
Output MP4 training video (5 min), voiceover, step-by-step visual graphics
Steps 5
Fallback Serve pre-rendered template video for equipment type; annotate with scenario-specific text overlays

LMS Content Agent

GoalCert Simulation Engine
Trigger Training video generation complete
Output Course ID, LMS course record, xAPI learning record seeded
Steps 6
Fallback Queue course for manual LMS staging; dispatch emergency notification with direct video link

Notification/Dispatch Agent

GoalCert Simulation Engine
Trigger LMS course status: READY; available technician in vicinity
Output Push notification, course invitation, dispatch confirmation, field navigation
Steps 7 → 9
Fallback Escalate to supervisor; broadcast to all available technicians in zone

GoalCert · AR Integration

Section 05
Critical Path & Latency SLAs
The connections on the critical path — with maximum allowed latency and why each matters for the end-to-end experience.
# Connection SLA Priority Why It Matters
1 Sensor → Digital Twin <1 sec CRITICAL Any delay here means the technician response is delayed proportionally. The clock starts at the sensor.
2 Digital Twin → Visualization <200ms CRITICAL SOC operator needs immediate visual feedback to assess severity and decide on escalation path.
3 Alert Router → Scenario Engine <3 sec CRITICAL Scenario generation is the gateway to training content — delay here ripples through the entire training pipeline.
4 Scenario Engine → Video Generation <10 sec CRITICAL Target is that the technician receives training within 15 seconds of alert. Video must begin rendering immediately.
5 Video Generation → LMS <5 sec CRITICAL Course must be READY before the notification is sent — technician should be able to start immediately on opening the link.
6 LMS → Technician Notification <2 sec HIGH Notification latency has a direct impact on total dispatch time.
7 AR Video Stream → SOC Expert <100ms CRITICAL Above 150ms the expert's annotations start to feel disconnected from the live video feed, creating guidance errors.
8 Expert Annotation → Technician Display <50ms CRITICAL AR overlay must feel instantaneous to the technician — any lag creates confusion about which physical component is being indicated.
9 Step Confirmation → Scenario Engine <2 sec CRITICAL Scenario engine must acknowledge step completion and serve next instruction before technician's attention drifts.
10 Maintenance Complete → Twin Resync <5 sec HIGH Twin must reflect OPERATIONAL status quickly so any concurrent monitoring dashboards and other technicians get accurate state.
11 Session Archive → Report Agent <60 sec STANDARD Report generation can tolerate moderate latency as it happens post-maintenance — target is delivery within 10 minutes of session close.
12 Knowledge Article → LMS Index <300 sec STANDARD Knowledge must be discoverable by the next technician encounter — same-shift indexing is acceptable.
